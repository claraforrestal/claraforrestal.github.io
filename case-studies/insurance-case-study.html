National Auto Insurance Provider

Mobile Application for Managing Auto Insurance Policies

Overview

A national insurance provider partnered with Levvel to iteratively research and design a brand-new mobile application for its customers. The objective of this new app was to substantially improve end-user Customer Experience and to allow the client to increase its market position across its portfolio companies.

Challenge

Based on their business objectives, the client identified both a timeline and a set of core application features prior to the engagement. Levvel was brought on with the goal of validating this initial set of features using customer research and user experience expertise. Through this iterative process, we identified and delivered designs for each of the most important features required for a minimum viable product.

The business-minded team at the client initially created the set of core features based on assumptions about what customers want and what competitors offer. Levvel was able to reframe the problem in a way that put the customer experience above all else. Our focus was on providing an application that customers would be delighted by and use.

Approach

The goal of Levvel’s engagement was to create the foundation artifacts required to support the creation of the MVP for the mobile application. This was broken up into three workstreams: research, product definition and visual design. The Levvel team consisted of 1 engagement manager, 2 researchers, 2 product designers, 1 business analyst, and 1 Xamarin developer.

Product Definition

The product definition phase began with a two-day product workshop. Levvel planned and led activities that were designed to help the entire project team come to consensus on the goals and success metrics for the project. This workshop also included several brainstorming exercises that resulted in a consolidated, prioritized list of user needs to be addressed by this new mobile application.

Product Design

Once we had determined the prioritized requirements list, there were about seven that both Levvel and the client agreed defined the minimum viable product. From there, Levvel’s product designers began the iterative process of designing prototypes and establishing a design system. Levvel’s product designers and business analyst collaborated with the stakeholders at the client through regular design reviews and requirement definition meetings. Each meeting gave the team further direction for the features of the new application.

Results

The client received a comprehensive set of designs, requirements and code to start implementation. Once development of this application is complete, customers of the client will be able to manage their Direct Auto car insurance policies from any smartphone. Making payments, accessing roadside assistance, and reviewing coverages are just some of the tasks users can perform right from their phones. The solution puts the client in a competitive position among other auto insurance providers while also enabling the client to serve customers in a more modern and immediate fashion.
